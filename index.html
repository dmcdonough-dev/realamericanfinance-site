Iron Pot Millionaire

Iron Pot Millionaire is a ruthless, disciplined approach to building wealth by stripping life down to what actually matters. It’s not about being cheap — it’s about being surgical.

Core Idea

You define the leanest, most focused version of your life — your "iron pot" baseline — and run that version long enough to radically change your financial trajectory.

How to Run It

  1. Define your non-negotiables: housing, food, basic transport, essential tools.
  2. Cut everything else for a defined season: subscriptions, lifestyle fluff, status spending.
  3. Stack cash aggressively and attack debt or build assets with intentional intensity.
  4. Track progress weekly, not emotionally — numbers only.

When to Use It

  • You're buried in debt and want out fast.
  • You need to build an emergency fund now, not someday.
  • You want to aggressively fund investments or a big opportunity.

Wealth Architecture

Wealth Architecture is about designing your financial life like a system, not a series of reactions. Every dollar has a job. Every account has a purpose. Nothing is random.

Three-Layer Model

  • Cash layer: emergency fund, operating cash, short-term reserves.
  • Defense layer: insurance, buffers, legal structure, risk management.
  • Offense layer: investments, income expansion, asymmetric opportunities.

How to Use It

  1. Map your current accounts and assets into the three layers.
  2. Identify gaps: missing emergency fund, weak defense, or no offense.
  3. Set clear targets for each layer (months of expenses, coverage levels, allocation percentages).
  4. Automate contributions so the system builds itself over time.

Scenario Planning

Scenario Planning gives you pre-built responses to the financial shocks most people never prepare for. When something hits, you don’t improvise — you execute.

Key Scenarios

  • Job loss: income disappears, expenses stay.
  • Market downturn: assets drop, emotions spike.
  • Unexpected expense: health, car, family, or legal hits.

How to Build Your Playbooks

  1. Define what “day 1, day 30, day 90” looks like for each scenario.
  2. Pre-decide what you will cut, sell, pause, or double down on.
  3. Tie each scenario to your Wealth Architecture layers so funding sources are clear.
  4. Review annually and adjust as your life and numbers change.
